Governance & Policies

  • Repository Governance Structure

    Open Research Systems Team

    The Open Research Systems (ORS) team is part of the Digital Initiatives within the Uganda Christian University. The ORS team is responsible for managing Veritus eCommons (Repository service) and technical aspects of the underpinning software platform, including integrations with other University systems.

    ORS Team Responsibilities:

    • Service ownership and management
    • Technical and software development
    • Platform maintenance and upgrades
    • System integrations
    • User support

    Digital Initiatives Directorate

    The Digital Initiatives Directorate brings together all teams responsible for delivering digital services. Multiple units collaborate on managing the technical infrastructure and operational aspects of Veritus eCommons.

    Repository Content Management

    Teams within the Office of Scholarly Communication manage and curate the repository's content. Open access submissions, research datasets, and doctoral theses are primarily deposited into Veritus eCommons through the University's CRIS (Current Research Information System).

    Infrastructure Support

    The technical infrastructure, including primary and backup storage servers, is managed by the Digital Services team with support from the University Information Services.

  • Repository Preservation Policies

    Digital Preservation Overview

    An overview of Veritus eCommons' digital preservation policy outlines how research outputs deposited in the repository are managed and preserved for long-term access.

    Repository Preservation Policy

    The preservation policy is part of a wider digital preservation strategy for institutional repositories and archives within the University.

    Preservation Scope

    Preservation policies determine:

    • How research outputs are managed after deposit
    • Storage and redundancy protocols
    • Format migration and normalization strategies
    • Metadata preservation and integrity
    • Access preservation over time

    Long-term Commitment

    The University is committed to ensuring that research outputs deposited in Veritus eCommons remain accessible and usable for future researchers and stakeholders.

  • Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs)

    What are DOIs?

    DOIs are persistent identifiers that provide a permanent, unique reference to digital research objects. Qualifying material deposited in Veritus eCommons may be assigned a DOI.

    DOI Assignment Criteria

    This policy outlines the criteria by which material is assessed - both in terms of the full text content and the associated metadata - and how requests to update material once a DOI has been granted are handled.

    Material Eligible for DOI Assignment:

    • Research publications (journals, conference proceedings)
    • Research datasets
    • Theses and dissertations
    • Technical reports
    • Software and code
    • Other scholarly outputs meeting quality standards

    Benefits of DOIs

    • Permanent, unique identification
    • Facilitates citation and attribution
    • Increases research visibility
    • Supports research evaluation and metrics
    • Enables tracking of research impact

    DOI Maintenance

    Once a DOI has been assigned, the University maintains the DOI metadata and ensures the persistent link remains active, even if the item location changes.

  • Repository Terms of Use

    Overview

    The Repository Terms of Use form the framework that determines how members of the University and the community of end-users interact with Veritus eCommons.

    Key Principles

    • Eligibility: Only research connected to Uganda Christian University may be deposited
    • Licensing: Content must be available under an appropriate open access or restricted license
    • Responsibility: Depositors are responsible for ensuring they have the right to deposit material
    • Preservation: The University commits to long-term preservation and access
    • Reuse: Users must respect licenses and provide appropriate attribution

    Rights and Responsibilities

    Repository Rights:

    • To accept or reject submissions
    • To remove material that violates terms of use or infringes rights
    • To preserve and migrate formats as needed
    • To assign persistent identifiers

    User Responsibilities:

    • To respect intellectual property rights
    • To comply with license terms
    • To provide accurate metadata
    • To report any rights infringements

  • Notice and Takedown Policy

    Purpose

    This policy outlines the process by which parties who feel that material deposited in Veritus eCommons infringes their rights may act to address the infringement.

    Filing a Notice

    If you believe material in the repository infringes your intellectual property rights, copyright, or other legal rights, you may submit a notice to the Repository team.

    Notice Should Include:

    • Your identification and contact information
    • Identification of the allegedly infringing material
    • Description of your rights and how they are infringed
    • Your good faith belief that the use is not authorized
    • A statement under penalty of perjury that the information is accurate
    • Your physical or electronic signature

    Response Procedure

    1. Repository team receives and reviews the notice
    2. Initial assessment for completeness and validity
    3. Contact depositor with infringement claim
    4. Opportunity for depositor to respond
    5. Determination of appropriate action
    6. Notification to all parties of outcome

    Possible Outcomes

    • Item removal from public view
    • Metadata modification
    • Access restriction
    • Claim dismissal if deemed invalid

    Contact Information

    Submit notice and takedown requests to: support@repository.ucu.ac.ug
