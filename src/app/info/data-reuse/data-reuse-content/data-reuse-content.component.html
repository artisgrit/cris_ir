How to Reuse Data

There are thousands of datasets in Veritus eCommons to discover and reuse in your research. If you are reusing any data from the repository (or any other third-party data), it is important to cite the dataset itself in your research and in any publications arising from reusing this data.

  • Finding Research Data in the Repository

    There are thousands of datasets in Veritus eCommons waiting to be discovered! There are also a growing number of other research output types such as software/code and methods.

    How to Search and Browse

    You can browse collections or search for data of interest in the repository by:

    • Title
    • Author
    • Subject
    • Type of data

    Discovery Beyond the Repository

    Repository content is also indexed on search engines such as Google, Google Scholar and Google Dataset Search, to help aid discovery by anyone anywhere.

    Data Availability Statements

    If you are depositing research data alongside a publication, most journals ask for a Data Availability Statement (DAS) to indicate how the data underlying the article can be accessed. This also helps with data discoverability and increases the visibility of your research outputs.

  • Reusing Research Data

    Data in the repository is available openly to download, unless temporarily under embargo (if associated with a research article prior to publication).

    License Information

    All data uploaded to the repository are given a license by the original depositor, which tells you what you can do with the data. You can find out which license is applicable under the heading 'Rights' on each repository item page.

    Understanding Licenses

    Most licenses chosen for datasets allow you to reuse the data as long as you credit (cite) the dataset (e.g., CC BY). As there are a variety of license options to choose from, it is important to check which one applies to the data you wish to use.

    Common License Types

    • CC BY (Attribution) - You must credit the original creators
    • CC BY-SA (Attribution-ShareAlike) - You must credit creators and share derivative works under the same license
    • CC BY-NC (Attribution-NonCommercial) - You must credit creators and cannot use for commercial purposes
    • CC0 (Public Domain) - No attribution required; freely reusable

    Important: Always check the specific license terms before reusing any dataset.

  • Why Cite Datasets?

    Datasets and software should be seen as research outputs in their own right. As such, it is important to cite any data you reuse (not just the associated publication) when you share or publish any work in the same way as you would for journal articles.

    Benefits of Data Citation

    • Gives credit to the original creators of the work
    • Increases discoverability of research
    • Helps with transparency and reproducibility
    • Builds trust within the research community
    • Helps other researchers understand your conclusions and acknowledge contributions

    How to Cite Datasets

    What to include in your citation may vary depending on the reference style used where you are publishing the work.

    Full Citation Should Include:

    • Author(s)
    • Publication date
    • Title
    • Place of publication (e.g., Veritus eCommons – Uganda Christian University Repository)
    • Digital Object Identifier (DOI)
    • Version number (if appropriate)

    Using Our Citation Tool

    To make citation easier, on each repository item page, under the heading 'Citation' there is a button to 'Copy citation' with a drop-down menu to choose from different referencing conventions (e.g., Harvard, APA, Chicago).

    Example Citation

    Smith, J., Johnson, K., & Williams, L. (2024). Climate data for East African Region [Data set]. Veritus eCommons – Uganda Christian University Repository. https://doi.org/10.25500/example
